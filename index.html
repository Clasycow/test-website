About Our Company

Pinnacle TCI, LLC is a training and consulting company. The owners and founders of Pinnacle TCI, LLC have almost four decades worth of law enforcement and investigative experience. After training law enforcement officers, detectives, and executives for years via other teaching capacities, the owners realized an even greater need for members of law enforcement to receive training on a much larger scale. The First 48 To The Final 12: From Crime Scene To Courtroom

Click Here To Register

Writing a comprehensive Case Report that meets the requirements of your law enforcement agency, protects your career as a law enforcement officer and withstands the pressures of courtroom examination can be a daunting prospect. In today’s world no law enforcement officer, or supervisor responsible for the review of case reports, should approach this task without proper training. Likewise, the culmination of your work as a police officer is presenting your case investigation in court. Understanding the dynamics of courtroom testimony is essential for any law enforcement officer. Pinnacle TCI, LLC has developed a two-day introductory course entitled Writing Effective Police Reports and Testifying in Court for patrol officers, detectives, and new supervisors to provide you the tools to be confident in your investigation reports and courtroom presentations.


⦿ Recognize the critical role how a case report plays not only in criminal prosecutions but to your career.
⦿ Understand the rulings of the United States Supreme Court cases, Brady v. Maryland, 373 U.S. 83 (1963) and Giglio v. United States, 405 U.S. 150 (1972) and how these cases impact law enforcement reports and your career as a law enforcement officers.
⦿ Understand the relationship between police reports and the legal Rules of Discovery.
⦿ Discuss the fundamentals of effective writing and practice the skills necessary to write effective reports.

⦿ Recognize in court how a law enforcement officer is viewed and how to project a positive image.
⦿ Understand the purpose of direct and cross examination questions.
⦿ Practice how to properly respond to direct and cross examination questions.
⦿ Develop the skills to present your case evidence in a visual trial.
⦿ Recognize the tactics employed by defense attorneys in court and how you should respond.
⦿ Recognize the most common mistakes in police reports and how you can avoid them.
⦿ Explore how your written case report impacts your courtroom testimony and how to prepare to testify as a witness in a criminal prosecution.

Child Abuse Investigations

Click Here To Register

Child abuse cases can be complex investigations and detectives have the important tasks of figuring out if abuse, whether sexual or physical is present and who is responsible. This two-day course will explore the various means of how these cases land on the desk of the case detective, what patrol can do to identify red flags within households and how to document any injuries. Attendees will learn about Forensic Interviews, who conducts them and how to work successfully with various outside agencies and stakeholders. Interviewing parents is one of the most important parts of a child abuse investigation, and students will learn how to interview parents and use those statements in conjunction with physical evidence to support the facts of the case and ultimately determine if a crime was committed and who committed it. Topics to be covered include:


⦿ Photographing Injuries
⦿ Beginning of a Child Abuse Investigation
⦿ Working a Joint Investigation
⦿ Patrol Duties at Child Abuse Scenes
⦿ Forensic Interviews
⦿ Interviewing Parents

⦿ Generating the Report/Note Taking
⦿ Receiving a Referral from Child Protective Services
⦿ CPS Follow-up Work
⦿ Investigation Gameplan
⦿ Multi-Disciplinary Teams (MDTs)
⦿ Child Custody Cases
